### 1. DEFINITIONS

Terms not defined herein have the meaning given in the Principal Agreement and applicable data protection laws.

1.1 **"Data Protection Laws"** means all applicable data protection and privacy legislation including the EU General Data Protection Regulation (2016/679) ("GDPR"), UK GDPR, California Consumer Privacy Act ("CCPA"), and any other applicable privacy law.

1.2 **"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person that is processed by Processor on behalf of Controller under the Principal Agreement.

1.3 **"Processing"** means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, recording, organization, storage, adaptation, alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

1.4 **"Data Subject"** means the identified or identifiable natural person to whom Personal Data relates.

1.5 **"Sub-processor"** means any third party engaged by Processor to process Personal Data on behalf of Controller.

1.6 **"Security Incident"** means any accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Personal Data.

### 2. SCOPE AND DETAILS OF PROCESSING

2.1 **Subject Matter:** Processing of Personal Data as necessary to provide the services under the Principal Agreement.

2.2 **Duration:** For the term of the Principal Agreement plus the period needed to delete or return all Personal Data.

2.3 **Nature and Purpose:** As described in Annex 1.

2.4 **Categories of Data Subjects:** {{DATA_SUBJECT_CATEGORIES: e.g., Controller's customers, employees, website visitors}}

2.5 **Types of Personal Data:** {{DATA_TYPES: e.g., name, email, IP address, usage data}}

2.6 **Special Categories:** {{SPECIAL_CATEGORIES: None / as specified in Annex 1}}

### 3. CONTROLLER OBLIGATIONS

3.1 Controller shall: (a) comply with Data Protection Laws in its use of the services and its instructions to Processor; (b) have a lawful basis for processing; (c) ensure that Data Subjects have been provided appropriate notice; and (d) obtain any necessary consents.

3.2 Controller is responsible for the accuracy, quality, and legality of Personal Data provided to Processor.

### 4. PROCESSOR OBLIGATIONS

4.1 **Instructions.** Processor shall process Personal Data only on Controller's documented instructions, including with regard to transfers to third countries, unless required by law. Processor shall inform Controller if it believes an instruction violates Data Protection Laws.

4.2 **Confidentiality.** Processor shall ensure that persons authorized to process Personal Data are subject to obligations of confidentiality.

4.3 **Security.** Processor shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Personal Data, as described in Annex 2, including:

(a) Pseudonymization and encryption of Personal Data where appropriate;
(b) Ability to ensure ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of processing systems;
(c) Ability to restore availability and access to Personal Data in a timely manner after an incident;
(d) Regular testing, assessing, and evaluating the effectiveness of security measures.

4.4 **Sub-processing.** Processor shall not engage Sub-processors without Controller's prior {{SUB_PROCESSOR_CONSENT: specific written / general written}} authorization.

- For general authorization: Processor shall maintain a current list of Sub-processors at {{SUB_PROCESSOR_LIST_URL}} and notify Controller at least {{SUB_PROCESSOR_NOTICE_PERIOD}} before engaging a new Sub-processor.
- Controller may object to a new Sub-processor within {{OBJECTION_PERIOD}} of notification. If Processor cannot accommodate the objection, Controller may terminate the affected services.
- Processor shall impose data protection obligations on Sub-processors no less protective than this DPA.
- Processor remains fully liable for Sub-processors' acts and omissions.

4.5 **Data Subject Rights.** Processor shall assist Controller in responding to Data Subject requests (access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction, objection) by appropriate technical and organizational measures.

4.6 **Assistance.** Processor shall assist Controller with: (a) data protection impact assessments; (b) prior consultations with supervisory authorities; (c) compliance with security obligations; (d) breach notification obligations — all taking into account the nature of processing and information available to Processor.

4.7 **Deletion/Return.** Upon termination of the Principal Agreement, Processor shall, at Controller's election, delete or return all Personal Data within {{DATA_RETURN_PERIOD}} and certify deletion in writing. Processor may retain copies only as required by applicable law, subject to continued confidentiality and security obligations.

4.8 **Audits.** Processor shall make available to Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA and allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, by Controller or an auditor mandated by Controller. Audits shall be conducted with {{AUDIT_NOTICE}} advance notice, during business hours, and no more than {{AUDIT_FREQUENCY}} unless a Security Incident has occurred.

### 5. SECURITY INCIDENT NOTIFICATION

5.1 Processor shall notify Controller of any Security Incident without undue delay and no later than {{BREACH_NOTIFICATION_PERIOD}} after becoming aware.

5.2 Notification shall include: (a) nature of the incident, including categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and records affected; (b) contact details for further information; (c) likely consequences; (d) measures taken or proposed to address and mitigate the incident.

5.3 Processor shall cooperate with Controller and take reasonable steps to mitigate the effects of the Security Incident.

5.4 Notification shall not be construed as an admission of fault.

### 6. INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS

6.1 Processor shall not transfer Personal Data outside the {{APPROVED_JURISDICTIONS: EEA / EEA and UK / specified countries}} without Controller's prior written consent and appropriate safeguards.

6.2 For authorized transfers, the Parties shall implement: {{TRANSFER_MECHANISM: Standard Contractual Clauses (attached as Annex 3) / Binding Corporate Rules / other approved mechanism}}.

6.3 If the chosen transfer mechanism is invalidated, the Parties shall cooperate to implement an alternative lawful mechanism.

### 7. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

7.1 Each Party's liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations set forth in the Principal Agreement, except as prohibited by Data Protection Laws.

7.2 Processor shall indemnify Controller for losses arising from Processor's breach of this DPA or Data Protection Laws, subject to the liability limitations in the Principal Agreement.

### 8. GENERAL

8.1 **Precedence.** In the event of conflict between this DPA and the Principal Agreement, this DPA prevails with respect to data protection matters.

8.2 **Governing Law.** {{GOVERNING_LAW}}

8.3 **Amendments.** Written and signed by both Parties.

8.4 **Severability.** Invalid provisions shall be replaced with valid provisions achieving the same purpose.

## ANNEX 2 — TECHNICAL AND ORGANIZATIONAL SECURITY MEASURES

Processor implements and maintains the following measures:

**1. Access Control**
- Role-based access control
- Multi-factor authentication for production systems
- Regular access reviews ({{ACCESS_REVIEW_FREQUENCY}})
- Principle of least privilege

**2. Encryption**
- Data in transit: TLS 1.2 or higher
- Data at rest: AES-256 encryption
- Key management procedures with regular rotation

**3. Network Security**
- Firewalls and intrusion detection systems
- Network segmentation
- Regular vulnerability scanning and penetration testing

**4. Physical Security**

**5. Organizational Measures**

- Security awareness training ({{TRAINING_FREQUENCY}})
