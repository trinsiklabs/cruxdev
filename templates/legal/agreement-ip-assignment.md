### 1. DEFINITIONS

1.1 **"Assigned IP"** means all intellectual property created, developed, conceived, or reduced to practice by Assignor, alone or jointly with others, that:

(a) Was created during the period of Assignor's relationship with Company and relates to Company's business, actual or demonstrably anticipated research, or development; or

(b) Was created using Company resources, facilities, or Confidential Information; or

(c) Is specifically described in Exhibit A.

1.2 **"Intellectual Property"** means all inventions, discoveries, ideas, improvements, designs, original works of authorship, trade secrets, know-how, software (including source code and object code), documentation, data, databases, processes, techniques, formulas, algorithms, specifications, drawings, and all related intellectual property rights including patents, copyrights, trademarks, and trade secrets.

1.3 **"Prior IP"** means intellectual property owned by Assignor before the relationship with Company, as listed in Exhibit B. Assignor represents that Exhibit B is complete.

### 2. ASSIGNMENT

2.1 **Grant of Assignment.** Assignor hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys to Company all right, title, and interest worldwide in and to the Assigned IP, including all intellectual property rights therein.

2.2 **Works Made for Hire.** To the extent any Assigned IP constitutes a copyrightable work, Assignor agrees that it is a "work made for hire" as defined by the U.S. Copyright Act. To the extent it does not qualify as a work made for hire, Assignor assigns all copyright to Company.

2.3 **Moral Rights.** Assignor waives all moral rights, rights of attribution, rights of integrity, and similar rights in the Assigned IP to the fullest extent permitted by law.

2.4 **Future Cooperation.** Assignor agrees to execute all documents and take all actions reasonably requested by Company to perfect, register, and enforce Company's rights in the Assigned IP, including patent applications, copyright registrations, and assignments. Assignor appoints Company as Assignor's attorney-in-fact for this purpose if Assignor is unable or unwilling to act.

2.5 **Retroactive Effect.** This assignment applies retroactively to all Assigned IP created during Assignor's relationship with Company, regardless of when this Agreement is signed.

### 3. PRIOR IP AND EXCLUSIONS

3.1 **Prior IP.** Assignor retains ownership of Prior IP listed in Exhibit B.

3.2 **License to Prior IP.** If any Prior IP is incorporated into Company products, services, or Assigned IP, Assignor grants Company a non-exclusive, perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free, irrevocable, sublicensable license to use, modify, and distribute such Prior IP as part of Company's products and services.

3.3 **Excluded IP.** The following are excluded from assignment, to the extent required by applicable law:
- Inventions developed entirely on Assignor's own time, without Company resources, unrelated to Company's business (where protected by state law such as California Labor Code Section 2870)
- Prior IP listed in Exhibit B

### 4. DISCLOSURE OBLIGATION

4.1 Assignor shall promptly disclose to Company in writing all inventions, improvements, and original works created during the relationship, whether or not Assignor believes they qualify as Assigned IP.

4.2 Company will determine, in good faith, whether disclosed items constitute Assigned IP.

### 5. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

5.1 **Assignor Represents:**

(a) Assignor has the full right and authority to make this assignment;

(b) The Assigned IP does not infringe any third-party intellectual property rights;

(c) Assignor has not previously assigned, transferred, licensed, or encumbered the Assigned IP;

(d) Exhibit B is a complete list of Prior IP that Assignor wishes to exclude;

(e) No third party has any claim or right to the Assigned IP;

(f) Assignor has not used any third-party intellectual property (including open-source software) in the Assigned IP without disclosure to Company.

### 6. CONFIDENTIALITY

6.1 Assignor shall maintain the confidentiality of all Assigned IP, trade secrets, and other Company confidential information during and after the relationship.

6.2 This obligation survives termination indefinitely with respect to trade secrets.
