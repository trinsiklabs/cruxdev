### RECITALS

The Parties wish to explore a potential business relationship concerning {{PURPOSE}} ("Purpose") and, in connection therewith, may disclose Confidential Information to each other. The Parties agree to protect such information as follows.

### 1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

1.1 "Confidential Information" means any and all non-public information disclosed by either Party ("Disclosing Party") to the other Party ("Receiving Party"), whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by inspection, including but not limited to:

- Business plans, strategies, and forecasts
- Financial data and projections
- Customer and supplier information
- Product plans, designs, and roadmaps
- Technical data, source code, algorithms, and architectures
- Trade secrets and know-how
- Marketing plans and pricing strategies
- Employee and contractor information
- Legal and regulatory matters
- Any other information identified as confidential at the time of disclosure

1.2 Confidential Information includes information disclosed by subsidiaries, affiliates, employees, contractors, and agents of the Disclosing Party.

1.3 Oral disclosures constitute Confidential Information if identified as confidential at the time of disclosure and summarized in writing within {{ORAL_CONFIRMATION_PERIOD: 10 business days}}.

### 2. EXCLUSIONS

Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

(a) Was publicly known at the time of disclosure or becomes publicly known through no fault of the Receiving Party;

(b) Was already in the Receiving Party's possession before disclosure, as evidenced by written records;

(c) Is received from a third party who is not under a confidentiality obligation to the Disclosing Party;

(d) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records; or

(e) Is approved for release by the Disclosing Party in writing.

### 3. OBLIGATIONS

3.1 **Protection.** The Receiving Party shall: (a) hold Confidential Information in strict confidence; (b) protect it using at least the same degree of care it uses for its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care; and (c) use it solely for the Purpose.

3.2 **Permitted Disclosures.** The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information only to its employees, directors, contractors, advisors, and agents ("Representatives") who: (a) need to know for the Purpose; and (b) are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Agreement. The Receiving Party is responsible for its Representatives' compliance.

3.3 **No Reverse Engineering.** The Receiving Party shall not reverse engineer, disassemble, or decompile any Confidential Information.

3.4 **No Copying.** The Receiving Party shall not copy or reproduce Confidential Information except as reasonably necessary for the Purpose.

### 4. COMPELLED DISCLOSURE

4.1 If the Receiving Party is compelled by law, regulation, or legal process to disclose Confidential Information, it shall: (a) provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice (to the extent legally permitted); (b) cooperate with the Disclosing Party's efforts to obtain a protective order or other remedy; and (c) disclose only the minimum information legally required.

4.2 Such compelled disclosure does not waive confidentiality for any other purpose.

### 5. TERM AND TERMINATION

5.1 **Agreement Term.** This Agreement remains in effect for {{AGREEMENT_TERM}} from the Effective Date, unless terminated earlier.

5.2 **Survival.** Confidentiality obligations survive termination for {{CONFIDENTIALITY_SURVIVAL_PERIOD}} from the date of disclosure of each item of Confidential Information. Trade secrets remain protected for as long as they qualify as trade secrets under applicable law.

5.3 **Termination.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon {{TERMINATION_NOTICE}} written notice.

### 6. RETURN OF MATERIALS

6.1 Upon termination or the Disclosing Party's written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly: (a) return all tangible materials containing Confidential Information; or (b) destroy all copies and certify destruction in writing.

6.2 The Receiving Party may retain one archival copy solely for legal compliance purposes, subject to continued confidentiality obligations.

6.3 Confidential Information in automated backup systems will be destroyed in accordance with the Receiving Party's standard backup rotation schedule.

### 7. NO RIGHTS GRANTED

7.1 No license or right under any patent, copyright, trademark, trade secret, or other intellectual property is granted by this Agreement.

7.2 Nothing in this Agreement obligates either Party to: (a) disclose any particular information; (b) enter into any further agreement; or (c) proceed with the Purpose.

7.3 Each Party reserves the right to independently develop products or services that may be similar to or competitive with those of the other Party.

### 8. NO WARRANTY

ALL CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION IS PROVIDED "AS IS." THE DISCLOSING PARTY MAKES NO WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, REGARDING THE ACCURACY, COMPLETENESS, OR FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE OF ANY CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION.

### 9. REMEDIES

9.1 Each Party acknowledges that a breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be inadequate.

9.2 The Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek equitable relief, including injunction and specific performance, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity.

9.3 The prevailing party in any legal action to enforce this Agreement shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees and costs.

10.5 **Severability.** Unenforceable provisions shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary.

10.6 **Waiver.** Failure to enforce a provision does not waive it.

10.7 **Assignment.** Neither Party may assign without prior written consent, except to a successor in connection with a merger or acquisition.
