### 2. FILING A DMCA TAKEDOWN NOTICE

If you believe content on our Service infringes your copyright, send a written notice to our designated agent containing:

1. **Your signature** — Physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or authorized agent.

2. **Identification of the copyrighted work** — Description of the copyrighted work you claim is infringed. If multiple works, provide a representative list.

3. **Identification of the infringing material** — Description of the material you claim infringes, with sufficient information for us to locate it (URLs, screenshots, etc.).

4. **Your contact information** — Name, address, phone number, and email address.

5. **Good faith statement** — A statement that you have a good faith belief that the use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.

6. **Accuracy statement** — A statement, under penalty of perjury, that the information in the notice is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on behalf of the owner.

### 3. HOW WE PROCESS NOTICES

Upon receiving a valid DMCA notice, we will:

1. **Acknowledge** the notice within {{ACKNOWLEDGMENT_PERIOD: 2 business days}}.
2. **Review** the notice for completeness and compliance with DMCA requirements.
3. **Remove or disable access** to the allegedly infringing material promptly.
4. **Notify the user** who posted the content, providing a copy of the notice (excluding your personal contact information if requested).
5. **Document** the notice and our response.

### 4. COUNTER-NOTIFICATION

If you believe your content was removed in error or that you have authorization to use the material, you may file a counter-notification containing:

1. **Your signature** — Physical or electronic.

2. **Identification of the removed material** — Description and location of the material before removal.

3. **Statement under penalty of perjury** — That you have a good faith belief the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification.

4. **Your contact information** — Name, address, phone number, and email.

5. **Consent to jurisdiction** — A statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the federal court in {{JURISDICTION}} and that you will accept service of process from the person who filed the original notice.

### 5. COUNTER-NOTIFICATION PROCESS

Upon receiving a valid counter-notification, we will:

1. **Forward** the counter-notification to the original complainant.
2. **Inform** the complainant that we will restore the material in 10 business days unless we receive notice that a court action has been filed.
3. **Restore** the material between 10 and 14 business days after receiving the counter-notification, unless the complainant files a court action.

### 6. REPEAT INFRINGER POLICY

We maintain a policy of terminating the accounts of repeat infringers in appropriate circumstances.

- **First offense:** Content removal and warning.
- **Second offense:** Content removal and final warning.
- **Third offense:** Account termination.

We may deviate from this policy in our sole discretion based on the severity and circumstances of the infringement.

### 7. MISREPRESENTATION WARNING

Under Section 512(f) of the DMCA, any person who knowingly materially misrepresents that material is infringing, or that material was removed by mistake, may be liable for damages including costs and attorneys' fees. Do not file a DMCA notice or counter-notification unless you are certain of your claim.
