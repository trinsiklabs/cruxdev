## COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST BY JURISDICTION

This checklist identifies key legal and regulatory requirements by jurisdiction. It is a starting point for compliance planning — not a substitute for local legal counsel.

---

### 1. HOW TO USE THIS CHECKLIST

1. Identify all jurisdictions where you: (a) have a legal entity; (b) have employees or contractors; (c) have customers; (d) store or process data; (e) market or sell products/services.
2. For each jurisdiction, work through the applicable section.
3. Mark each item: **Done** / **In Progress** / **N/A** / **Needs Counsel**.
4. Review quarterly or when entering new markets.

### 2. UNITED STATES — FEDERAL

#### 2.1 Business Formation
- [ ] Entity registered (LLC, C-Corp, S-Corp, etc.)
- [ ] EIN (Employer Identification Number) obtained
- [ ] Operating agreement / bylaws adopted
- [ ] Registered agent designated
- [ ] Business licenses obtained (federal, if applicable)

#### 2.2 Tax
- [ ] Federal tax elections filed (e.g., S-Corp election via Form 2553)
- [ ] Quarterly estimated tax payments scheduled
- [ ] Annual tax return filing process established
- [ ] 1099 / W-9 process for contractors
- [ ] Sales tax nexus analysis performed
- [ ] State tax registrations completed (income, sales, payroll)

#### 2.3 Employment
- [ ] I-9 (Employment Eligibility) process
- [ ] W-4 collection for employees
- [ ] Workers' compensation insurance
- [ ] OSHA compliance (workplace safety)
- [ ] FLSA compliance (minimum wage, overtime)
- [ ] ADA compliance (reasonable accommodations)
- [ ] FMLA compliance (if 50+ employees)
- [ ] Anti-discrimination policies (Title VII, ADA, ADEA)
- [ ] EEO-1 reporting (if 100+ employees)
- [ ] Labor law postings (federal and state)

#### 2.4 Data Privacy
- [ ] CCPA/CPRA compliance (if serving California residents)
- [ ] State privacy law compliance (Virginia VCDPA, Colorado CPA, Connecticut CTDPA, etc.)
- [ ] Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) if applicable
- [ ] HIPAA compliance (if handling health data)
- [ ] FERPA compliance (if handling education data)
- [ ] GLBA compliance (if financial services)
- [ ] FTC Act Section 5 (unfair/deceptive practices) compliance

#### 2.5 Intellectual Property
- [ ] Trademark search and registration (USPTO)
- [ ] Copyright registration for key works
- [ ] Patent strategy assessment
- [ ] Trade secret protection program
- [ ] IP assignment agreements with employees/contractors
- [ ] Open source license compliance

#### 2.6 Industry-Specific
- [ ] SEC compliance (if securities involved)
- [ ] FinCEN compliance (if money transmission)
- [ ] FDA compliance (if applicable)
- [ ] FCC compliance (if telecommunications)
- [ ] Export control (EAR, ITAR) compliance

### 4. EUROPEAN UNION / EEA

#### 4.1 GDPR Compliance
- [ ] Lawful basis for processing identified for each processing activity
- [ ] Privacy policy published (compliant with Articles 13-14)
- [ ] Data Processing Agreements with all processors (Article 28)
- [ ] Record of Processing Activities maintained (Article 30)
- [ ] Data Protection Impact Assessments conducted where required (Article 35)
- [ ] Data Protection Officer appointed (if required by Article 37)
- [ ] Data Subject rights processes implemented (access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction, objection)
- [ ] Cookie consent mechanism (ePrivacy Directive compliant)
- [ ] Data breach notification process (72-hour requirement, Article 33)
- [ ] International transfer mechanisms in place (SCCs, adequacy decisions)
- [ ] Privacy by design and default implemented (Article 25)
- [ ] Staff training on data protection

#### 4.2 Business Operations
- [ ] EU entity established (if required for local presence)
- [ ] EU representative designated (Article 27, if no establishment)
- [ ] VAT registration (in each applicable member state or OSS registration)
- [ ] Consumer protection compliance (Consumer Rights Directive)
- [ ] Distance selling regulations compliance
- [ ] Accessibility requirements (European Accessibility Act)
- [ ] Digital Services Act compliance (if applicable)
- [ ] AI Act compliance (if deploying AI systems in the EU)

#### 4.3 Employment (EU-wide principles)
- [ ] Employment contracts compliant with local law
- [ ] Working time regulations compliance
- [ ] Equal treatment and anti-discrimination
- [ ] Works council / employee representation (if applicable)
- [ ] Posted workers regulations (if sending employees across borders)
