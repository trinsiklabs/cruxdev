## {{COMPANY_NAME}} CODE OF CONDUCT

**Effective Date:** {{EFFECTIVE_DATE}}

This Code of Conduct applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, officers, and directors of {{COMPANY_NAME}} ("Company"). It sets expectations for professional behavior and ethical decision-making.

---

### 1. OUR PRINCIPLES

1. **Integrity.** We are honest and transparent in all dealings. We do the right thing even when no one is watching.
2. **Respect.** We treat everyone with dignity, fairness, and empathy. We value diverse perspectives.
3. **Accountability.** We take ownership of our actions and their consequences. We deliver on commitments.
4. **Excellence.** We hold ourselves to high standards and continuously improve.
5. **Trust.** We protect the trust placed in us by customers, partners, and each other.

### 2. WORKPLACE ENVIRONMENT

#### 2.1 Harassment-Free Workplace
We maintain a workplace free from harassment, bullying, and intimidation. Prohibited conduct includes:
- Sexual harassment (unwelcome advances, requests for favors, verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature)
- Verbal harassment (slurs, epithets, offensive jokes, derogatory comments)
- Physical harassment (unwanted touching, blocking movement, physical intimidation)
- Visual harassment (offensive images, gestures, displays)
- Cyberbullying (harassing messages, social media attacks, doxxing)

#### 2.2 Discrimination-Free Workplace
We do not tolerate discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, pregnancy, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, marital status, veteran status, or any other legally protected characteristic.

#### 2.3 Inclusive Communication
- Use inclusive language
- Respect pronouns and preferred names
- Be mindful of cultural differences
- Listen actively and make space for all voices

### 3. PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT

#### 3.1 Conflicts of Interest
- Disclose any relationship or activity that could conflict with Company interests
- Do not use your position for personal gain at the Company's expense
- Outside employment, board service, or significant financial interests in competitors, suppliers, or customers require disclosure and approval
- Romantic relationships with direct reports must be disclosed to {{HR_CONTACT}}

#### 3.2 Gifts and Entertainment
- Do not accept gifts valued over ${{GIFT_THRESHOLD}} from vendors, customers, or partners
- Modest business meals and entertainment are acceptable
- Never accept cash or cash equivalents
- Government officials: special restrictions apply — consult {{LEGAL_CONTACT}}

#### 3.3 Anti-Corruption and Anti-Bribery
- Never offer, promise, or pay bribes to anyone
- Never accept bribes from anyone
- Comply with the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act, UK Bribery Act, and all applicable anti-corruption laws
- Report any solicitation of bribes immediately

#### 3.4 Fair Competition
- Compete fairly and ethically
- Do not engage in price-fixing, market allocation, or bid-rigging
- Do not misappropriate competitor trade secrets
- Make honest claims about our products and services

### 4. PROTECTING INFORMATION

#### 4.1 Confidential Information
- Protect Company, customer, and partner confidential information
- Share only on a need-to-know basis
- Use approved tools and channels for sensitive communications
- Confidentiality obligations continue after separation from the Company

#### 4.2 Data Privacy
- Handle personal data in compliance with applicable privacy laws
- Collect only what is necessary
- Store securely and access only for legitimate business purposes
- Report any data breaches or privacy incidents immediately

#### 4.3 Intellectual Property
- Company IP belongs to the Company (per your employment/contractor agreement)
- Respect third-party IP — do not copy, use, or distribute without proper authorization
- Report any known or suspected IP infringement

#### 4.4 Company Assets
- Use Company resources (equipment, software, funds) for business purposes
- Limited personal use is acceptable per the Acceptable Use Policy
- Protect against theft, waste, and abuse

### 5. FINANCIAL INTEGRITY

- Maintain accurate and complete financial records
- Never falsify records, expense reports, or time sheets
- Follow expense and approval policies
- Cooperate fully with audits and investigations

### 6. HEALTH AND SAFETY

- Follow all safety policies and procedures
- Report unsafe conditions or practices
- Do not come to work (or work remotely) while impaired
- Violence and threats of violence are prohibited

### 7. ENVIRONMENTAL RESPONSIBILITY

- Minimize waste and environmental impact
- Comply with all environmental laws and regulations
- Consider sustainability in business decisions

### 8. SOCIAL MEDIA AND PUBLIC COMMUNICATIONS

- Do not speak on behalf of the Company without authorization
- Identify personal opinions as your own, not the Company's
- Do not disclose confidential information on social media
- Be professional and respectful in all public communications

### 9. REPORTING CONCERNS

#### 9.1 How to Report
If you witness or experience a violation of this Code, report it through any of these channels:
- Your manager
- {{HR_CONTACT}}
- {{ETHICS_HOTLINE}} (anonymous option available)
- {{LEGAL_CONTACT}}

#### 9.2 No Retaliation
The Company strictly prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith or participates in an investigation. Retaliation is itself a violation of this Code and will result in disciplinary action.

#### 9.3 Investigation
All reports will be investigated promptly, thoroughly, and confidentially (to the extent possible). You may be asked to participate in an investigation — cooperation is expected.

### 10. ENFORCEMENT

Violations of this Code may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment. The severity of the response depends on the nature and circumstances of the violation. Illegal conduct may be referred to law enforcement.
